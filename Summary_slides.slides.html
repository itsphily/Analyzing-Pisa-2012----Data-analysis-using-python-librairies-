Do all students compete on an equal footing ?¶

THE ANALYSIS¶

I will try to answer this question by analyzing factors that are outside of the student's control, and visualizing their impact on the grades of the students.

Perhaps the most obvious one is the impact of the parent'socio-economic characteristics on the student's grades. The second aspect,which was popularized by Malcom Gladwell in his book outliers, is the relationship between the student's age and his grades. Finally, we are going to visualize the impact of having the mother and/or the father at home.

The analysis will be done with data coming from the 2012 PISA dataset.

THE PISA DATASET¶

PISA is a survey of students' skills and knowledge as they approach the end of compulsory education. It is not a conventional school test. Rather than examining how well students have learned the school curriculum, it looks at how well prepared they are for life beyond school.

Around 510,000 students in 65 economies took part in the PISA 2012 assessment of reading, mathematics and science representing about 28 million 15-year-olds globally. Of those economies, 44 took part in an assessment of creative problem solving and 18 in an assessment of financial literacy.

The impact of the parent's socio-economic characteristics on the student's grades.¶

Higher Socio-economic status -- higher grades¶

The average SQ ISEI whether the mother's, the father's or combined (a quantitative proxy for the socio economic status) is correlated grades.
Perhaps even more interestingly, is that the combined SQ ISEI has a smaller interquartile range, or in other words is more tightly correlated with the grades.

What is SQ ISEI ?¶

The Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA) International Socio-Economic Index of Occupational Status (ISEI) was derived from students responses on parental occupation. The index captures the attributes of occupations that convert parents education into income. The index was derived by the optimal scaling of occupation groups to maximise the indirect effect of education on income through occupation and to minimise the direct effect of education on income, net of occupation (both effects being net of age).

The impact of having the mother and/or the father at home.¶

Engineered features¶

I created two variables sense_of_belonging (which I will use as a proxy for social affinity) and perceived_control (which I will use as a proxy for internal locus of control). This was meant to simply to be able to more easily compare these metrics with the presence of the father or mother at home.

Mother/Father where are you ?¶

Students with either mother or father at home have an increased sense of belonging and perceived control. Social affinity and high internal locus are usually described as traits of of highly succesful individuals.

Mother and Father always make me do my homework!¶

A higher proportion of students with either their mother or father at home have higher grades. In general, the presence of the father or the mother at home have a positive impact on the grades of the students.

Conclusion¶

Similar students born in different months will have different grades. And this is only one of the factors that contributes to the inequality between students. Despite being aware of those inequalities, we still insist on grading kids on a standard scale. Even worse we have a standard model of intelligence, and kids who don't abide are forced to take drugs such as ritalin to fit into a mold.

It is time for us to learn how to teach!¶
